Segmentation

How the Rules Change by Segment

The agent shift lands differently depending on what you sell. Where you sit determines which of Sharp's laws bends first.

Product Businesses

Physical goods, where the feed becomes the shelf.

FMCG

Examples: P&G, Unilever

  • Recommendation algorithms — win the engine, not the shelf glance
  • Subscription & replenishment — the default agent basket
  • Shelf visibility → feed visibility
  • Brand power → data power (feeds, reviews, claims)

Durable Goods

Examples: automotive, electronics, appliances

  • Comparison agents — do the spec work a showroom used to
  • Configuration agents — build and price the option set
  • Ecosystems — lock-in shapes what the agent surfaces
  • Service revenues — the durable margin after commoditised hardware

Service Businesses

Where the customer's agent negotiates with yours.

Travel

  • AI trip planners
  • Dynamic packaging
  • Agent-to-agent booking

Insurance

  • AI brokers
  • Claims automation
  • Product comparison

Banking & Wealth

  • Personal finance agents
  • Automated switching
  • Advice agents

Gartner expects AI agents to intermediate more than $15 trillion of B2B purchasing by 2028 — services are not insulated by their complexity; that complexity is exactly what agents are built to handle.
