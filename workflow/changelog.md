### Notable findings this cycle

- **Adobe Q1 2026 data (cb-c1-02):** AI-sourced traffic to US retail sites grew 393% YoY and now converts 42% *better* than non-AI traffic—a complete reversal from 12 months earlier. Most retail sites remain insufficiently machine-readable to capitalise.
- **Mastercard Agent Pay for Machines (tc-c1-02):** Launched June 10, 2026 with 30+ partners; enables AI-to-AI micro-transactions at machine speed, permissions stored on blockchain. Represents the full disintermediation endpoint where brand trust must be encoded at the protocol layer.
- **Forrester reality check (rt-c1-03):** Published May 28, "most agentic experiences are merely assistive, not truly autonomous"—hype outpaces behaviour; independent marketplaces most at risk from answer-engine traffic leakage.
- **UK CMA guidance (rg-c1-01):** First authoritative regulatory framework (March 9, 2026): businesses fully liable for agent behaviour; disputes on agent-initiated card transactions run at 2.4× the rate of human-initiated ones (Clifford Chance, rg-c1-03).
- **IMF Note (rg-c1-04):** IMF proposes three-layer payment architecture—agent intent layer must remain separated from deterministic settlement—signalling agentic commerce is now a financial-stability concern.
- **McKinsey Europe (br-c1-01):** 50%+ of European consumer journeys already influenced by LLMs or AI search; autonomous execution is "coming" but not yet dominant; supports the thesis timeline.
- **Amazon AWS (rt-c1-01):** Opens its Rufus-derived AI shopping tech to external retailers (~$12B incremental sales track record); agent commerce infrastructure becoming a managed service any retailer can subscribe to.
- **Deloitte APAC (cb-c1-03):** 29% of APAC consumer businesses already adopting agentic AI, expected to surge to 76% within two years; machine-readable catalogue identified as the pivotal differentiator.
- **Watch-source Mollie:** Still no verifiable agentic commerce content; kept on watchlist for Cycle 2.
